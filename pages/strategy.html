Investment Philosophy

Our Strategy

Long-only. Fundamentally driven. Collectively accountable. This is how we invest.

The Foundation

Long-Only Equity

ECI is a pure long-only fund. We do not short, we do not use leverage, we do not trade derivatives. Our conviction sits entirely in identifying companies that will be worth more in the future than they are today.

This constraint is deliberate. It forces intellectual rigour — you cannot hedge your way out of a bad idea. Every position we build must stand on its own merits, backed by independent research and a clearly articulated thesis that the full team has challenged.

The Edge

Bottom-Up Fundamentals

We start with the company, not the macro. Before we form any market view, we build a detailed understanding of a business: its economics, competitive positioning, management quality, and valuation.

Macro factors inform the context but never drive the decision. A great company at a fair price — understood deeply — will outperform over a full cycle regardless of short-term noise.

What We Look For

Five Investment Pillars

Every pitch we evaluate is judged against five criteria. A strong investment should score well on all five.

1

Competitive Moat

What protects the business from competition? We look for durable advantages — brand, network effects, switching costs, cost leadership, or regulatory barriers — that sustain pricing power over time.

2

Quality of Earnings

Reported profits mean nothing without cash conversion. We prioritise businesses with high free cash flow generation, clean accounting, and earnings that are recurring and predictable rather than cyclical or one-off.

3

Attractive Valuation

Even the best business is a bad investment if bought at the wrong price. We anchor our target price to a DCF and validate it against comparable multiples — always asking whether the current price implies a margin of safety.

4

Clear Catalyst

Why now? A pitch must identify a specific catalyst — earnings inflection, regulatory change, product launch, management transformation, or re-rating — that will close the gap between intrinsic value and market price within a defined horizon.

5

Understood Risks

Every investment has risks. What we demand is that the analyst has identified them, sized them, and explained why the upside compensates for them. A risk we have named and quantified is one we can manage.

The ECI Standard

"Would a professional fund manager approve this thesis?" is the bar we hold ourselves to. We do not accept vague theses, incomplete models, or unchallenged assumptions. Every pitch is improved by the team before a vote is taken.

How We Decide

The Investment Process

From first idea to live position — every step is structured, collegial, and documented.

Step 1 — Idea Generation
Analysts identify candidate stocks through sector screens, thematic research, or news flow. The idea is presented informally to a team leader before proceeding.
Step 2 — Deep Research
The analyst builds a financial model (DCF + comps), reads all relevant filings, and writes a structured research note covering the business, thesis, risks, and valuation.
Step 3 — Pitch Presentation
The full pitch is presented to the ECI team in a weekly session. Questions are rigorous — the team challenges every assumption, every number, every risk omission.
Step 4 — Revision & Refinement
Based on team feedback, the analyst refines the model and thesis. A second presentation may be required for complex or contentious pitches.
Step 5 — Vote & Sizing
The bureau votes on inclusion. If approved, the team determines position size based on conviction level and portfolio construction rules.
Step 6 — Monitoring & Exit
Every position is reviewed quarterly against the original thesis. If the thesis breaks or the target price is reached, the team votes on exit. Performance is reported against benchmark.

Risk Management

Portfolio Construction Rules

Concentration creates alpha. Over-diversification creates a closet index. We aim for a focused, high-conviction portfolio with clear risk guardrails.

Position Sizing

  • — Core positions: 8–12% of portfolio
  • — Standard positions: 4–7%
  • — Exploratory positions: 1–3%
  • — Single-stock max: 15%

Diversification Rules

  • — Minimum 4 sectors represented at all times
  • — Single sector cap: 40%
  • — Geographic diversification encouraged
  • — Cash target: 5–10% at all times

Exit Discipline

  • — Target price reached → exit review
  • — Thesis invalidated → immediate review
  • — Better opportunity identified → reallocation vote
  • — Quarterly review mandatory for all positions

Benchmark

  • — Primary: EURO STOXX 600 (total return)
  • — Secondary: CAC 40 (total return)
  • — Reported quarterly, disclosed publicly
  • — Absolute and relative alpha tracked
